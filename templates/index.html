System Architecture

The Universal Knowledge Graph is built on a 13-axis architecture that provides a comprehensive framework for knowledge representation and reasoning.

Axis 1: Knowledge

The foundational knowledge axis with Pillar Levels 1-100 that provides a comprehensive classification framework for all knowledge domains.

Axis 2: Sectors

Industry sectors and organizational domains that contextualize knowledge within specific professional and operational environments.

Axis 3: Domains

Specialized knowledge domains that represent established fields of study, practice, or expertise within broader sectors.

Axis 4: Concepts

Abstract ideas, theories, and conceptual frameworks that form the building blocks of knowledge across domains and disciplines.

Axis 5: Entities

Concrete objects, organizations, individuals, and other distinct entities that exist in the real world or conceptual spaces.

Axis 6: Regulatory

Legal, compliance, and regulatory frameworks that govern activities, practices, and standards across different jurisdictions and domains.

Axis 7: Compliance

Standards, best practices, and compliance requirements that ensure adherence to regulatory frameworks and industry benchmarks.

Axis 8: Processes

Procedures, workflows, and systematic approaches to achieving specific outcomes or managing operations across domains.

Axis 9: Relations

Connections, associations, and relationships between different knowledge elements, entities, concepts, and other components.

Axis 10: Time

Temporal dimensions of knowledge, including historical context, chronological development, and time-based relationships.

Axis 11: Applications

Practical implementations, use cases, and applied frameworks that put knowledge into action for specific purposes.

Axis 12: Location

Spatial and geographic dimensions of knowledge, including regional variations, location-specific contexts, and spatial relationships.

Axis 13: Interface

User interaction points, communication channels, and interfaces for accessing, interacting with, and contributing to the knowledge system.

Core Engine Components

Simulation Engine

An in-memory nested layered simulation system that models complex interactions between knowledge elements across the top three layers of the UKG architecture:

  • Layer 1: Knowledge Diffusion - Simulates how knowledge spreads and activates across Pillar Levels
  • Layer 2: Sector Influence - Models how sectors adapt and contextualize knowledge from different pillars
  • Layer 3: Domain Specialization - Represents specialized transformations of knowledge within specific domains

Knowledge Graph Manager

A sophisticated graph database management system that handles the persistent storage and retrieval of UKG components:

  • Nodes - Knowledge elements across all 13 axes
  • Edges - Relationships and connections between nodes
  • Algorithms - Knowledge processing and transformation methods
  • Structured Memory - Organized storage of processed information

Built on PostgreSQL with advanced graph capabilities for efficient querying and traversal.
